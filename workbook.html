The Better Question

The question I hear most often is: “How do I get what I want in life?” For years I thought that was the right question. It isn’t.

The better question is: How do I get what I want, hold onto it, and enjoy it? Some people get what they want and lose it. Others get it and keep it but never truly enjoy it. Still others never get close because they unknowingly work against themselves.

What I discovered over the years is that many of the obstacles we face are not outside of us. They are often hidden patterns inside of us — patterns of thinking, feeling, and behaving that we may not even realize are influencing us.

This workbook is designed to help you identify some of those patterns and begin creating solutions that really work. Not perfect solutions. Not magic solutions. Practical solutions.

The goal is not perfection. The goal is awareness. Because awareness creates choices. And choices create change.

— Gary Jay

Identifying Your Hidden Roadblocks
Roadblocks are often hidden in plain sight. When we identify them, we gain options. When we ignore them, they continue influencing our lives. Check every roadblock that feels relevant to you right now.
The Wedding Ring Story

“Imagine sitting in a dark movie theater. You drop your wedding ring. Unable to find it, you walk into a brightly lit lobby and begin searching there.”

Someone asks: “Why are you looking here?” You answer: “Because the light is better.” The problem is that the ring is still in the theater.

Many people spend years searching for answers where it is easiest to look rather than where the answer actually exists.

Your Internal Thermostat
Have you ever noticed how some people seem to return to the same place over and over again? Different relationship. Same problems. Different job. Same frustrations. An invisible thermostat may be at work.

Your internal thermostat is the invisible set point that pulls you back to familiar settings — even when you don’t want to be there. It’s shaped by beliefs, assumptions, habits, and expectations that keep “resetting the dial.”

Too Hot Zone
Overwhelmed, burned out, reactive. Pushed beyond comfort to the point of breakdown.
Comfort Zone
Familiar but stuck. Safe but unfulfilling. Where most people spend most of their time.
Growth Zone
Where real change happens. Slightly uncomfortable but rich with possibility and momentum.

Which area of your life keeps returning to the same level?
